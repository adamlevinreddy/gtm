# **Reddy Account-Based Marketing Playbook**

**Flow:** Company Identified → Build Contact List → Organize & Prioritize → Get Approval → Enroll in Campaigns 

---

## **Purpose**

This document defines the repeatable process for running account-based outreach at Reddy. When a target company is identified, this is the step-by-step for how we build the contact list, get approval, and get contacts into campaigns.

**This is a continuous engine, not a one-time project.** Every new target company runs through the same process.

---

## **Step 1 — Company Identified**

**Definition:** A target company enters the pipeline. This is the trigger for everything that follows.

**Common sources:**

* Website visitor flagged in analytics  
* Adam or leadership flags a company  
* Conference lead or inbound signal  
* ABM list addition

Qualification:  
	Must preliminarily meet our ICP

---

## **Step 2 — Build Contact List**

**Definition:** Pull relevant contacts from Apollo across three function areas.

**Key Actions:**

* Search the target company in Apollo  
* Pull contacts in three categories:  
  * **L\&D** — Learning and Development, Training \- CX Specific ideally  
  * **QA** — Quality Assurance, Quality Management \- CX Specific ideally  
  * **Operations** — Customer Service, Customer Experience  
* Export contacts **without revealing emails** — this preserves Apollo credits until contacts are approved  
* Export the following fields: **Name, Title, Company**

---

## **Step 3 — Organize & Prioritize**

**Definition:** Structure the raw contact list into a reviewable spreadsheet.

**Key Actions:**

* Create a spreadsheet with **per company** and put the sheet in the Marketing / ABM folder by company name.  
* Add a **Priority column** — rank each contact:  
  * **1** — Strong fit (title is clearly relevant to L\&D, QA, or Operations leadership)  
  * **2** — Possible fit (related title, worth including)  
  * **3** — Weak fit (include only if list is thin)  
* Delete contacts that are not a good target.  
* Add a **Campaign column** — assign each contact to one of three campaigns: **L\&D**, **QA**, or **Operations**  
* Sort each sheet by priority, with the strongest contacts on top

\* The sorting by priority can be dropped in the future once we have a good sense for what are good contacts.  This is just a learning step at the moment

---

## **Step 4 — Get Approval**

**Definition:** Submit the spreadsheet to leadership for review to identify top targets. (Before spending credits or sending any outreach)

**Key Actions:**

* Send the completed spreadsheet to Tom and/or Adam  
* Leadership reviews the list, cuts contacts that do not make sense, and confirms top priorities  
* Once approved, go back into Apollo and **reveal emails and Linkedin URLs only for approved contacts** — this is when credits are spent

Do not move to Step 5 until the list is approved.

---

## **Step 5 — Enroll in Campaigns**

**Definition:** Approved contacts are loaded into existing HubSpot sequences (“Newly Interested”) and LinkedIn outreach begins.

**Key Actions:**

* Export approved contacts as a CSV from Apollo (now with emails and linkedin URLs revealed)  
* Upload the CSV into HubSpot  
* Enroll each contact into the appropriate **existing sequence** based on their campaign tag:  
  * L\&D sequence  
  * QA sequence  
  * Operations sequence  
* Send LinkedIn connection requests to approved contacts via Sales Connector or manually
